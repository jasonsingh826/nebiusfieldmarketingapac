◆ Nebius APAC Marketing Strategy

Two goals. Everything ladders up to them.

This plan applies the regional marketing framework to Nebius's specific situation — a strong product entering a competitive market where brand preference, not category awareness, is the gap to close.

01
The two goals
Nebius enters APJ with a $2B NVIDIA investment, a $20B+ contract backlog, and the best inference platform in the neocloud category. APJ's AI infrastructure buyers are increasingly aware of the neocloud alternative to hyperscalers — but Nebius has limited brand recognition in this region compared to CoreWeave and the established cloud providers. There's no existing regional marketing function, limited local customer evidence, and no established partner ecosystem. This plan exists to fix that.
Goal 1 — Demand creation

Become the primary neocloud brand in Asia Pacific.

APJ's AI buyers know what neoclouds are. What they don't know is why Nebius should be their first choice. Goal 1 is a pure demand creation play — building brand preference, thought leadership positioning, and ecosystem credibility so that Nebius is the name at the top of the list.

Goal 2 — Demand creation + demand capture

Acquire net new logos.

Brand preference creates the conditions; logo acquisition is the outcome. Goal 2 runs both motions in parallel — demand creation to build executive relationships, and demand capture to convert active buying intent into signed customers.

The flywheel

Brand preference makes sales conversations easier and shortens deal cycles. New logos create customer proof that accelerates brand credibility. Brand-heavy in months 1-6, shifting toward logo acquisition as awareness compounds.

02
How we'll operate

NVIDIA is the brand shortcut.

$2B investment + Reference Platform status = the most powerful credibility signal in APJ. Lead with the NVIDIA relationship in every market entry motion. A trust accelerant that compresses the timeline from unknown entrant to credible contender.

Sales-embedded from day one.

Join every pipeline call. Co-design account campaigns with AEs. Build programs that sales actively pulls into deals. In a new region with a small team, every program must serve a live deal or a named target account.

Sequence markets, don't spread thin.

Singapore first — home base, dense AI startups, fastest path to logos. Japan second — largest IT market, requires in-language investment. Korea and India expand as commercial hires come online.

Take global playbooks and make them real. Build what central can't.

Adapt central positioning for local competitive landscapes and language. Build from scratch where central will never go: sovereign AI for ASEAN, Japan trust-building programs, Indian startup pricing narratives.

Demand capture
Programs that convert existing buying intent into pipeline and closed deals through targeted, use-case-specific outreach.

Outbound ABM campaigns

What we do
  • 30 Tier 1 accounts (1:1 campaigns), 150 Tier 2 (cluster by vertical)
  • Campaigns by use case: inference cost for fintech, open-source models for media, sovereign AI for government
  • Jointly activated with sales through in-office mini events, dinners, hospitality (8-12 attendees)
  • Weekly account reviews with AEs
  • Multi-stakeholder engagement across buying committee
  • Salesforce pipeline tracking and attribution
Why it mattersHighest-conversion pipeline motion. Use case specificity = higher lead quality. In-person activations convert digital engagement to relationships. Joint activation ensures every touch serves a live deal.

Partner co-sell

What we do
  • NVIDIA co-sell: joint pipeline reviews, shared account targeting, co-funded demand gen
  • SI activation: 3-5 partners (Accenture, NTT Data, Infosys, TCS, Samsung SDS)
  • Partner playbooks, joint solution architectures, MDF allocation
  • Quarterly partner business reviews
  • ISV alliances (DataRobot, Hugging Face, Anyscale): joint webinars, mutual co-sell
Why it mattersPartners bring existing customer trust and relationships. NVIDIA referral = evaluation half done. SI recommendation outweighs direct marketing. $1 spend unlocks $2-3 in partner co-investment.
05
Who we're targeting
Three buyer segments. AI-native startups are the fastest logos. Digital enterprises are the biggest. Public sector is the longest play but most defensible.

AI-native startups

Entry: Token Factory

Buyer: Technical founders, CTOs. Short cycles.

Trigger: Inference cost wall, self-hosting open-source models, scaling past API limits.

Serves: Fast logos (Goal 2) + community advocates (Goal 1)

Digital enterprises

Entry: AI Cloud then Token Factory

Buyer: VP Eng, CTO, Head of AI/ML. Procurement involved.

Trigger: Cloud repatriation (cost), sovereign AI mandates, open-source model shift.

Serves: High-value logos (Goal 2) + brand credibility (Goal 1)

Public sector & sovereign AI

Entry: AI Cloud (dedicated)

Buyer: CIO, Digital Transformation Lead. SI-led.

Trigger: National AI funding, data residency regulations, sovereign compute mandates.

Serves: Category-defining logos (Goal 2) + strategic positioning (Goal 1)
