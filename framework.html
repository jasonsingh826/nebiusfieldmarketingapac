◈ Framework

Regional field marketing operating framework

A company-agnostic model for building and running B2B field marketing in complex multi-market regions. Refined across five "first marketing hire" roles — from managed cloud to enterprise SaaS to AI infrastructure.

01
The regional marketing dual mandate
Regional field marketing operates at the intersection of two forces: central playbooks that need to be localized and regional realities that demand net-new programs. The best regional marketers are translators and builders who do both simultaneously.
Localize from central

Take global playbooks and make them work in-region

Adapt messaging for local buyer psychology, translate or rebuild content for cultural fit, select locally relevant proof points and case studies, and map global campaigns to regional event calendars and buying cycles.

Build region-specific

Create programs that only make sense locally

Government-led sovereign AI initiatives that require bespoke positioning. Partner ecosystems that are entirely regional. Cultural formats — executive dinners in Japan, roundtables in Singapore, community meetups in India — that central's playbook doesn't cover.

The localization spectrum

Not everything needs the same level of adaptation. Technical benchmarks travel globally with minimal change. Competitive positioning needs market-specific framing. Customer stories need to be locally sourced. Event formats need to be culturally designed from the ground up. The job is knowing which lever to pull for each asset.

02
Buyer awareness & the two marketing motions
B2B marketing runs two fundamentally different motions. Treating them as one — or measuring them the same way — is the most common failure mode in field marketing. In a new-to-region context, the balance tips heavily toward demand creation first, then shifts as brand awareness builds.
Demand creation

Build awareness and shape buying intent before it exists

Audience: Unaware and problem-aware buyers who don't yet know the brand. In a new region, this is the majority of the market.

Motions: Thought leadership, category education, developer community, event speaking, co-marketing with ecosystem partners, earned media. Measured by reach, engagement, and share of voice.

Regional nuance: Demand creation content must be rebuilt for APJ, not just translated. Partner with regional analysts, tech media, and developer communities.

Demand capture

Convert existing buying intent into pipeline and deals

Audience: Solution-aware and product-aware buyers actively evaluating options. In a new region, this is a small but high-value slice.

Motions: ABM, search/intent programs, sales enablement, competitive displacement, executive roundtables, partner co-sell. Measured by pipeline generated, conversion rates, and deal velocity.

Regional nuance: ABM account lists need regional intelligence. Sales alignment must account for timezone spread. Partner co-sell requires in-region relationship building.

03
ICP tiering & market prioritization
Not all markets or accounts deserve equal investment. ICP tiering determines where you concentrate resources, while market prioritization determines geographic sequencing.
Tier 1 — Strategic

High-touch, named accounts

1:1 ABM. Custom campaigns per account. Dedicated sales alignment. Executive engagement. Typically 20-50 accounts in-region.

Tier 2 — Growth

Cluster-based engagement

1:few ABM. Campaigns grouped by vertical, use case, or market. Shared content with targeted personalization. 100-300 accounts.

Tier 3 — Scale

Programmatic & community-led

1:many. Digital programs, developer community, self-serve content, partner-led engagement. 1,000+ accounts.

04
Buying committee & persona mapping
Enterprise purchases involve multiple stakeholders with different motivations. Field marketing must reach each member of the buying committee, not just the technical evaluator. Decision-making structures vary significantly across APJ markets.

Technical evaluator

Titles: ML Engineer, Platform Engineer, AI Architect

Cares about: Benchmarks, API compatibility, model library, migration path, developer experience

Programs: Hands-on workshops, hackathons, benchmarks, developer docs, community meetups

APJ nuance: Developer communities are highly localized. Content must be in-language for Japan/Korea.

Technical decision maker

Titles: VP Engineering, CTO, Head of AI/ML

Cares about: TCO, reliability, vendor lock-in risk, scaling trajectory, architecture fit

Programs: Architecture deep-dives, TCO calculators, customer roundtables, executive briefings

APJ nuance: In Japan and Korea, trust-building takes longer — multi-touch over months, not one event.

Business sponsor

Titles: CEO/COO (startups), CIO/CDO (enterprise)

Cares about: Speed to market, cost structure, competitive edge, AI strategy alignment

Programs: ROI frameworks, business impact stories, executive dinners, analyst validation

APJ nuance: C-suite responds to peer-led content — regional CEO/CTO testimonials carry more weight than global case studies.

Procurement & compliance

Titles: CISO, Legal, Procurement, Data Protection Officer

Cares about: SOC 2, ISO 27001, data residency, HIPAA, SLA guarantees

Programs: Compliance docs, security whitepapers, reference architectures, audit reports

APJ nuance: Data sovereignty is the dominant concern. This persona has effective veto power.

The localization operating model
The most common failure in regional marketing is treating localization as translation. Real localization is a content operating system that determines what to take from central, what to adapt, and what to build from scratch.

The content operating rhythm

Establish a biweekly cadence with central product marketing: review upcoming launches, flag what needs regional adaptation, share regional market intelligence back to central. This is a partnership where the region contributes insights that make global content better, and central provides raw materials the region refines.

06
Compelling events & timing triggers
Compelling events create urgency. They push a buyer from "interesting" to "we need to act." Field marketing should map programs to these triggers.

Regulatory & policy shifts

Data sovereignty mandates, AI governance frameworks, sector-specific compliance deadlines. When regulations change, procurement timelines compress.

Technology inflection points

New GPU launches, open-source model releases, platform paradigm shifts (agentic AI). These create "evaluate now" moments.

Competitive displacement

Contract renewals, price increases, service degradation, vendor acquisitions. When the incumbent stumbles, capture programs become high-ROI.

Budget & planning cycles

Japan's fiscal year starts April 1. India's starts April 1. Most SEA enterprises follow calendar year. Align campaigns to when money is allocated.

Organizational changes

New CTO/CIO appointments, AI team expansions, digital transformation mandates. Leadership changes trigger vendor re-evaluation.

Scale thresholds

When inference costs cross a pain threshold, when GPU waitlists delay launches. These "cost shock" or "speed shock" moments create organic demand.

Measurement architecture
Each motion gets measured on its own terms. Measuring demand creation by pipeline generated is like measuring brand advertising by click-through rate — it optimizes for the wrong behavior.

The revenue connection

Every metric should trace back to a revenue impact story, even if the connection is indirect. Developer community growth → increased product evaluations → pipeline. The measurement system exists to help leadership make investment decisions — show them the path from activity to revenue.
