Framework

Regional field marketing operating framework

A company-agnostic model for building B2B field marketing in complex multi-market regions.

01
The dual mandate

Localize from central

Adapt global positioning for local buyer psychology. Translate content for cultural fit. Map campaigns to regional calendars. Select locally relevant proof points.

Build region-specific

Programs central will never build. Government initiatives. Regional partner ecosystems. Cultural formats — executive dinners in Japan, roundtables in Singapore, meetups in India.

02
Two marketing motions
Demand creation

Build awareness before intent exists

Thought leadership, category education, developer community, event speaking, co-marketing, earned media. Measured by reach, engagement, share of voice.

Demand capture

Convert existing buying intent

ABM, search/intent programs, sales enablement, competitive displacement, executive roundtables, partner co-sell. Measured by pipeline, conversion, deal velocity.

03
ICP tiering
Tier 1 — Strategic

20–50 named accounts

1:1 ABM. Custom campaigns. Executive engagement. Named AE alignment. Highest pipeline, longest cycles.

Tier 2 — Growth

100–300 accounts

1:few ABM. Campaigns by vertical or use case. Targeted personalization. Faster cycles, higher volume.

Tier 3 — Scale

1,000+ accounts

1:many. Digital programs, developer community, self-serve content, partner-led. Widest reach.

04
Buying committee

Technical evaluator

ML Engineers, AI Architects. Benchmarks, APIs, migration path. Workshops, hackathons, docs. In-language for JP/KR.

Technical decision maker

VP Eng, CTO, Head of AI. TCO, reliability, lock-in risk. Architecture deep-dives, roundtables. Trust-building takes months in JP/KR.

Business sponsor

CEO/COO, CIO/CDO. Speed-to-market, cost, competitive edge. ROI frameworks, exec dinners, analyst validation. Peer testimonials win in APJ.

Procurement & compliance

CISO, Legal, DPO. SOC 2, data residency, SLAs. Compliance docs, security whitepapers. Veto power. Sovereignty is #1 concern.

06
Compelling events

Regulatory shifts

Data sovereignty, AI governance, compliance deadlines. Compresses procurement.

Tech inflections

New GPUs, open-source models, agentic AI. Creates "evaluate now" urgency.

Competitive displacement

Renewals, price hikes, vendor acquisitions. Capture the dissatisfied.

Budget cycles

JP/IN FY April 1. SEA calendar year. Align to when money is allocated.

Org changes

New CTO/CIO, AI team growth. Leadership changes trigger re-evaluation.

Scale thresholds

Inference costs cross pain point. GPU waitlists. "Cost shock" = organic demand.
