Part I · Field marketing, generally
I
The Framework.

How I run field marketing — agnostic of company, product, or region. The structure I bring into any new market. Part II applies it specifically to Nebius APJ.

§ 01 / My Approach

Three things,
repeated every time.

A field marketing plan lives or dies on three calls: where to concentrate the budget, how tightly marketing is bonded to sales, and how much of central's playbook is rewritten locally. I treat all three the same way every time.

01 · Concentrate

Pick three. Fund them hard.

Three signature bets that get 80%+ of the spend. Everything else still runs, but doesn't get the headcount or the executive attention. The math forces the discipline.

02 · Embed

Marketing inside sales.

The capture motion is co-owned with AEs and SDRs from day one. Account lists, invite lists, follow-up SLAs. Marketing as a sales function, not an adjacent department.

03 · Localize

Adapt, don't translate.

Central positioning gets rewritten by people on the ground. Trust signals, event formats, language register — all set locally. The framework holds; the vehicle changes.

§ 02 / Two Outcomes

Every field plan,
two outcomes.

There are only two outcomes that matter. One builds the brand. One converts it into logos. Every program lives under one or the other — never both, never neither.

Outcome 01
Demand Creation

Build the brand.

Make the company the name buyers reach for. Voice, advocacy, and presence at the places where buyers and analysts already are. Measured in share of voice, recall, and conversations initiated.

Outcome 02
Demand Capture

Sign the logos.

Convert brand into pipeline. Sales-embedded programs with relationship depth no webinar or banner can match. Measured in pipeline value, signed logos, and win-rate by motion.

§ 03 / Operating Philosophy
Do fewer
things perfectly.

Most field plans entering a new market spread themselves across twenty programs. I run three, and own them. Three bets, executed with intensity, that become what the company is known for in the region. Everything else still happens — but those three I crush.

§ 05 / Anatomy of a Bet

What makes a bet,
a bet.

"Bet" is a strict label. It's not "a thing we're doing" or "a focus area." It's a program that meets four tests at once. Everything that doesn't meet all four lives in the foundation.

Test 01

Multi-program.

Cuts across at least three channels at once — PR, events, content, ABM, partners. Not a single tactic. A theme that runs through multiple programs.

Test 02

Multi-quarter.

A 4+ quarter horizon. Compounds over time. Anything resolvable in a single quarter is a campaign, not a bet.

Test 03

Owns one KPI.

A single top-line metric the bet is accountable for. Pipeline. Recall. Advocates signed. Not a basket of stats — one number.

Test 04

≥ 25% of budget.

Each bet gets at least a quarter of the year's spend. Three bets × 25% = the 80% concentration. The number forces the discipline.

§ 06 / Operating Principles

Four ways
I actually run.

The framework is what we do. These are how we do it — the operating principles that govern every call inside the framework.

01

Speed is strategy.

Market windows open and close. Every conversation started inside a window carries weight that won't exist outside it. Move first; refine in motion.

02

Find the force multiplier.

One channel — a partner, a platform, a borrowed brand — usually returns more than the next ten combined. Find it, lean on it, scale around it.

03

Sales-embedded.

Every pipeline call. Co-designed campaigns. Capture programs jointly owned with AEs and SDRs. Marketing and sales as one team, not adjacent functions.

04

Localize, don't translate.

Central positioning gets adapted by people on the ground. Trust signals, event formats, language register — all rewritten locally. The framework holds.

§ 11 / How I'd Run This

The framework's principles,
applied to Nebius.

The four universal principles from Part I, rendered concrete for Nebius APJ. Each one names the specific move I'd make.

Speed

Ride the momentum.

Nebius is trending — in media, analyst coverage, customer conversations. Every conversation started this year carries weight that won't exist once the market normalizes. Move now.

Multiplier

NVIDIA is the channel.

$2B investment plus Reference Platform status. Co-present at every event, co-sell into their base. Primary credibility channel — borrow it shamelessly.

Embed

Hospitality is jointly owned.

Every pipeline call. Co-designed campaigns. The hospitality program is jointly owned with AEs and SDRs from invite list to follow-up SLA. Not a marketing program with sales attendance.

Localize

Build where central can't.

Adapt central positioning for local markets. Build from scratch where central can't reach: sovereign AI for ASEAN, Japan trust-building, India pricing.
